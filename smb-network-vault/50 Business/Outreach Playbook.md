# Outreach Playbook

Client acquisition — **the actual hard part**. Activity targets live in [[Weekly Quotas and Metrics]]; the offer you lead with is the [[Security Snapshot]].

## The funnel

```
List building → Outreach (calls/walk-ins/email) → 15-min conversation
→ Wedge: $495 Security Snapshot → Findings meeting → Retainer proposal
```

## Channels, ranked by expected yield

1. **Your existing network (weeks 1–4).** Every former colleague, employer, vendor contact, friend-of-friend who owns or manages a business. Script: *"I've started a fractional IT practice for small businesses. Who do you know that's always fighting their computers?"* The first 1–3 clients almost certainly come from here.
2. **Referral partners (start immediately, compounds forever).** Accountants, **insurance brokers** (especially cyber — you make their clients insurable, they hand you [[Cyber Insurance Questionnaire|questionnaires]]), business bankers, commercial realtors (new offices need networks → [[New Site Deployment]]), managed-print and phone-system vendors. Offer 10% of first-year revenue or flat $500 per closed client. **Five active partners eventually replaces cold outreach entirely.**
3. **Walk-ins/cold calls.** Build a list from Google Maps by vertical ([[Target Verticals]]), 20 min research each, then call or walk in. **Ask for the office manager, not the owner** — the office manager *is* the current IT department and feels the pain daily.
4. **Local networking** — chamber of commerce, BNI or similar (only if the IT seat is open), vertical industry associations.
5. **Minimal web presence** — one-page site + Google Business Profile. For validation when a referral checks you out, **not** lead generation. One weekend max in year 1.

## Scripts

**Cold call/walk-in opener:**

> "Hi — I run a local IT practice for offices your size. Quick question: when a computer breaks or email stops working here, who fixes it?"
> *(Whatever they answer:)* "That's what I usually hear. I do a flat $495 security check — backups, email, the stuff cyber insurance asks about — and give the owner a two-page report. Worst case you learn you're in good shape. Who would I talk to about that?"

**Top objections:**

- *"We have a guy."* → "Most of my clients had a guy. I'm not here to replace him tomorrow — the snapshot just tells you if anything important is slipping through. If your guy's doing great, the report will say so."
- *"Too small to be hacked."* → "The attacks aren't targeted — they're automated, and small offices are the soft targets. Also, your insurance renewal is going to ask these questions either way."
- *"No budget."* → "The retainer's a later conversation. This is $495 once. One ransomware day costs more than a decade of me."

When asked how you compare to a real MSP, answer honestly → [[Fractional IT vs Full MSP]].
