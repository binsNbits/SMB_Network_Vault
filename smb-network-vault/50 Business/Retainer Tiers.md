# Retainer Tiers

Price **per-user per-month with a floor**, so pricing scales with the client and quotes take 30 seconds. **Never bill hourly for ongoing work** — hourly punishes you for being fast and makes clients afraid to call. One-time work is priced separately → [[Project Pricing]].

## CORE — $95/user/month (minimum $600/month)

- Unlimited remote helpdesk (business hours; best-effort same day, guaranteed < 4 business hours)
- Patch management & endpoint monitoring on all covered machines
- Managed antivirus/EDR ([[Tool Stack Upgrade Path]])
- M365/Google Workspace administration — users, licenses, groups, security defaults ([[M365 vs Google Workspace Strategy]])
- Employee onboarding/offboarding (accounts, devices, access removal)
- **Vendor management** — you call the ISP/copier/software vendor so they don't have to
- Monthly one-page report: what happened, what was fixed, what's at risk
- Keep-lights-on networking on existing gear → scope boundary in [[Retainer Scope vs Project Scope]]

## SECURE — $135/user/month (minimum $900/month)

Everything in Core, plus:

- Managed backup for M365/Workspace data + **quarterly test restores** ([[Backup Strategy]])
- Password manager deployment and management
- MFA enforced everywhere; conditional access baseline
- Phishing simulation + short security training (quarterly)
- [[Cyber Insurance Questionnaire]] support — filled out with them, annually
- Annual written security summary (doubles as the CPA **WISP** for [[Target Verticals|accounting clients]])

## Fences

- **On-site:** 1 included visit/month on Secure; otherwise $125/visit. Keep visits **scheduled, not on-demand**, or they eat the calendar ([[Solo Operations and Boundaries]]).
- **After-hours emergencies:** $185/hr, 1-hour minimum. A fence, not a revenue line.

## Pricing rules

1. **Never discount the monthly rate** — extend onboarding or waive a project fee instead. MRR is the business's value.
2. Annual price increase of **4–6% written into the agreement** from day one ([[Legal Insurance and Licensing]]).
3. If a prospect haggles hard on $600/month, **walk**. Price-grinders are also the worst-behaved clients ([[Risks and Failure Modes]]).
