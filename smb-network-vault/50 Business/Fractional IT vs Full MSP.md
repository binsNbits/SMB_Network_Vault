# Fractional IT vs Full MSP

This practice uses MSP *mechanics* (flat retainer, standardized RMM/EDR/backup stack, per-user pricing — [[Retainer Tiers]], [[Launch Tool Stack]]) with fractional-IT *positioning* (your part-time, local IT person). Be honest about the difference — prospects and referral partners will ask.

| | Full MSP | This practice |
|---|---|---|
| **What the client buys** | A service organization with SLAs across their whole estate | A known person's shared time and expertise, backed by real tooling |
| **Team** | NOC, tiered helpdesk, account managers | One operator — solo ceiling ~12–15 clients ([[Solo Operations and Boundaries]]) |
| **Coverage** | Often 24/7 monitoring and response | Business hours, <4-hr SLA; after-hours at $185/hr is a fence, not coverage |
| **Security depth** | May include a SOC / managed detection team | Hardening + EDR + backups + [[Cyber Insurance Questionnaire]] support — *not* a monitored SOC |
| **Redundancy** | Team absorbs sickness and vacations | Solo fragility is real — mitigated by [[Documentation Standards|documentation]] and a mutual-cover handshake ([[Risks and Failure Modes]]) |
| **Right-sized client** | 30–500+ seats | 5–30 employees — the segment full MSPs can't serve profitably with a human touch ([[Ideal Client Profile]]) |

## What to say when asked

> "A big MSP gives you a ticket queue and a rotating cast of techs. I give you one person who knows your office, backed by the same class of tools. If you grow past ~30 seats or need 24/7 coverage, you'll outgrow me — and I'll tell you when that happens."

That last sentence closes more deals than it loses; small-business owners are allergic to being oversold.

## What never to claim

- 24/7 monitoring
- A security operations center
- Guaranteed after-hours response
- Team redundancy

Overclaiming is both a sales risk and an **E&O liability** ([[Legal Insurance and Licensing]]) — your defensibility is your paper trail, and that includes marketing claims.
