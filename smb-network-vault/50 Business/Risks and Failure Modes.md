# Risks and Failure Modes

1. **Not doing outreach — the #1 killer.** Delivery always feels more urgent than selling. The [[Weekly Quotas and Metrics|Friday quota review]] exists for exactly this. Skip outreach for three weeks and the business is already failing — you just won't see it for four months.
2. **The runway problem.** No capital + 3–6 month sales cycles. Mitigated by keeping other income and front-loading project revenue ([[Financial Model]]).
3. **One anchor client dependency.** A 10-person business is tempted to become "basically an employee" of its first big client. **Cap any client at 25% of revenue**; keep prospecting even when busy.
4. **Underpricing.** $400/month clients cost the same overhead as $1,200 ones. Hold the **$600 floor** from day one ([[Retainer Tiers]]).
5. **Security incident at a client.** It will eventually happen. This is why: E&O insurance, **written backup declines**, MFA-by-default, and documented recommendations they declined ([[Legal Insurance and Licensing]], [[Security Incident Basics]]). **Your defensibility is your paper trail** — including marketing claims ([[Fractional IT vs Full MSP]]).
6. **Solo fragility (you get sick).** Document everything from client #1 as if a stranger had to cover ([[Documentation Standards]], [[Client Onboarding Runbook]]); establish a **mutual-cover handshake** with another local solo MSP by month 6.
7. **Commoditization from AI/self-healing IT.** Real trend, slow at SMB scale. Durable moats: physical locality, trust, vendor-wrangling, and being a human who answers. Lean into the security/insurance angle — that demand is regulatorily driven and growing ([[Cyber Insurance Questionnaire]]).
