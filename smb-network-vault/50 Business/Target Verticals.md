# Target Verticals

Pick 2–3 to focus on. All must still fit the [[Ideal Client Profile]].

| Vertical | Why they buy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Law firms | Confidentiality obligations, cyber-insurance pressure, billable-hour pain when IT breaks | Pay well, demand responsiveness |
| Accounting/CPA firms | **IRS WISP requirement** (written security plan is legally mandated), seasonal urgency | The WISP is a ready-made wedge → [[Cyber Insurance Questionnaire]] |
| Dental/medical/veterinary | HIPAA anxiety, practice-management software dependence | Often locked to vertical software vendors — you become the translator |
| Construction/trades offices | Field + office tech chaos, zero internal IT | Less price-sensitive than you'd expect |
| Property management | Lots of vendors and portals, high email volume | Automation upsell potential |
| Nonprofits | Google/Microsoft **nonprofit licensing** saves them real money on day one ([[M365 vs Google Workspace Strategy]]) | Lower rates but low churn and great referrals |

> [!tip] The niching rule
> After the first 3–4 clients, notice which vertical is easiest **for you** and double down. "IT for law firms in [county]" beats "IT services" in every future sales conversation ([[Outreach Playbook]]).
