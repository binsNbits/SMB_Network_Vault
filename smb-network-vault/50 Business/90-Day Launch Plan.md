# 90-Day Launch Plan

## Days 1–14 — Foundation

- [ ] Form LLC; open business bank account; set up invoicing + ACH auto-pay → [[Legal Insurance and Licensing]]
- [ ] Stand up the free stack → [[Launch Tool Stack]]; write the MSA/SOW (template + one review pass)
- [ ] Define the [[Security Snapshot]] checklist + 2-page report template
- [ ] One-page website + Google Business Profile + business email
- [ ] Write the list: 50 network contacts + 100 local businesses in 2–3 [[Target Verticals]]

## Days 15–45 — Outreach reps

- [ ] Contact all 50 network contacts — the "who do you know" script ([[Outreach Playbook]])
- [ ] Begin weekly quotas: 40 touches, 10 conversations ([[Weekly Quotas and Metrics]])
- [ ] Approach 5 potential referral partners: 2 accountants, 2 insurance brokers, 1 banker
- [ ] **Goal: 3 Security Snapshots sold or given.** Deliver them exceptionally — these are the case studies
- [ ] Buy E&O insurance at first paying engagement

## Days 46–90 — Convert and systematize

- [ ] Run findings meetings; pitch retainers — **goal: 1–2 signed retainers + 1–2 projects** ([[Retainer Tiers]], [[Project Pricing]])
- [ ] Execute the [[Client Onboarding Runbook]]; write documentation as you go ([[Documentation Standards]])
- [ ] Ask every happy contact: *"Who else should I be talking to?"* — referrals start now, not later
- [ ] Friday metrics review, **every week, no exceptions** ([[Weekly Quotas and Metrics]])
- [ ] **Day-90 checkpoint:** ≥1 retainer + pipeline of 5 = on track. Zero revenue + <300 total touches = the problem is activity, not the model ([[Risks and Failure Modes]]).

## What NOT to do in year 1

- Don't build an app, a SaaS, or "productize" anything. Sell hours-that-look-like-outcomes on retainers.
- Don't take clients outside the service area "just this once" ([[Ideal Client Profile]]).
- Don't offer full-service SEO/marketing — technical SEO audits only, as paid add-ons to existing clients.
- Don't hire until you're turning away work at 12+ retainers ([[Solo Operations and Boundaries]]).
- Don't spend on ads. Every marketing dollar in year 1 is better spent as an hour of outreach.
