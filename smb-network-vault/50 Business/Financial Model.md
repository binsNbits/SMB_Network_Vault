# Financial Model

## Assumptions

- Average retainer: **$1,100/month blended** (mix of Core/Secure — [[Retainer Tiers]])
- Project revenue: ~$1,500 average; roughly 1 project per new client + 1 per 4 existing clients per quarter ([[Project Pricing]])
- Retainer churn: ~10%/year — SMB IT retainers are sticky **if you answer the phone**
- Costs: tools ~10–12% of revenue ([[Launch Tool Stack]]), insurance ~$100/mo, misc ~$150/mo

## Ramp — conservative vs. base case

| Milestone | Conservative | Base case |
|---|---|---|
| First paying client (project or snapshot) | Month 2 | Month 1 |
| First retainer | Month 3 | Month 2 |
| 3 retainers (~$3.3K MRR) | Month 8 | Month 5 |
| 6 retainers (~$6.6K MRR) | Month 14 | Month 9 |
| 10 retainers (~$11K MRR) — **a living** | Month 22 | Month 14 |
| 12–15 retainers (solo ceiling, ~$13–17K MRR — [[Solo Operations and Boundaries]]) | Month 30+ | Month 20–24 |

Add roughly $1,000–2,500/month of project revenue on top of MRR from month 4 onward.

## Year-1 P&L, base case (approximate)

- Revenue: ~$55–75K (MRR ramp + projects)
- Costs: ~$6–9K (tools, insurance, LLC, fuel, misc)
- **Owner take: ~$48–66K** — thin but survivable; year 2 at 10–12 retainers clears $120K+ gross on the same cost base.

## The no-capital survival constraint

Months 1–6 are lean. Mitigations, in order of preference:

1. **Keep current income** — outreach on evenings/lunches + delivery on flex time until ~$4–5K MRR (roughly doubles the odds of success).
2. Front-load [[Project Pricing|project work]] (migrations, network builds) for cash even though retainers are the goal.
3. Collect retainers **first-month-in-advance** and projects **50% up front** — standard practice, and it *is* your working capital.

Tracked weekly against the [[Weekly Quotas and Metrics|Friday dashboard]]; behind-plan MRR is always fixed at the activity level.
