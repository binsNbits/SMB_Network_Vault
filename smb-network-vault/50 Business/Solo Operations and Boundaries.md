# Solo Operations and Boundaries

## Standardization is the margin

Every client gets the **same stack** — same RMM agent, same EDR, same backup, same password manager, same documentation template ([[Launch Tool Stack]], [[Documentation Standards]]). Custom environments are how solo MSPs drown. If a prospect insists on exotic tooling: **price it 40% higher or decline**. (The productivity suite — M365 vs Google — is the allowed exception → [[M365 vs Google Workspace Strategy]].)

## Time budget (steady state, per Core client)

- ~2–5 hours/month reactive + ~1 hour proactive/reporting. The [[Retainer Scope vs Project Scope]] boundary exists to protect this budget.
- **Solo ceiling: ~12–15 retainer clients** (≈200–300 endpoints) alongside project work. At avg $1,200/client that's $14–18K MRR — decide *then* whether to hire a junior tech or hold at capacity and raise prices.

## Boundaries that keep this a good life

- Business-hours support with a defined SLA (< 4 business hours). **No free after-hours** — the $185/hr fence handles real emergencies ([[Retainer Tiers]]).
- Maintenance windows and site visits are **scheduled and batched, never ad hoc**.
- One **"office day" per week** with no client meetings: documentation, invoicing, outreach ([[Weekly Quotas and Metrics]]).
- **Fire the bottom client once a year.** There will always be one generating 40% of the pain for 8% of the revenue.

A client consistently over 10 hrs/month gets a price increase or a root-cause [[Project Pricing|project]] — the metric is on the [[Weekly Quotas and Metrics|Friday dashboard]]. Solo fragility itself is a named risk with named mitigations → [[Risks and Failure Modes]].
